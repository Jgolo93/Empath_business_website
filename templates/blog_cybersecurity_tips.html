1 Implement Strong Password Policies

Weak passwords are one of the most common entry points for hackers. Ensure your team uses strong authentication practices:

  • Minimum 12 characters - Longer passwords are exponentially harder to crack
  • Mix character types - Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Unique per account - Never reuse passwords across different services
  • Regular rotation - Change passwords every 90 days minimum
💡
Pro Tip: Use a password manager like LastPass or Bitwarden to securely store and generate complex passwords. This eliminates the need to remember dozens of passwords while maintaining security.

2 Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA adds an extra layer of security by requiring users to provide two or more verification factors. This simple step can prevent 99.9% of automated attacks.

Enable MFA on all critical business accounts:

✓ Email accounts (Gmail, Outlook)
✓ Banking and financial systems
✓ Cloud services (Microsoft 365, Google Workspace)
✓ Social media accounts

3 Regular Software Updates and Patching

Outdated software is a goldmine for cybercriminals. Keep your systems secure with these practices:

  • Install Windows/macOS updates immediately when available
  • Update antivirus software definitions daily
  • Patch business applications weekly
  • Replace unsupported hardware and software promptly

5 Regular Data Backups

Ransomware attacks are on the rise in South Africa. Protect your business with a comprehensive backup strategy:

The 3-2-1 Backup Rule

3 Copies of your data
2 Different media types
1 Off-site location
  • Daily automated backups to multiple locations
  • Both cloud and local backup solutions
  • Regular backup testing and verification
  • Encrypted backup storage
